# Assumptions

## Data access
- **No internal data access.** This proposal is based on the documents shared in the *KC Research* and *Rate Cards* folders, plus Nielsen NFI SA fan study (March 2025), kaizerchiefs.com, and publicly-available rate cards (DMS March 2026, Mediamark Sept 2025, OnsideZA, Polygon Network).
- **No KC Mobile core-system access.** Subscriber, churn, and ARPU figures are not in this site. Where the plan references activations or sell-through, it is based on the targets in the V3 brief.

## Strategy
- **Directional only.** The media mix, supplier choices and tactical recommendations are directional. Final supplier rates need to be re-quoted at the moment of booking — rate cards are starting points.
- **Wholesale-led.** The plan assumes the V3 brief's 80% physical / 20% eSIM split holds, and that Vodacom remains the host network and a co-funded retail partner.
- **Football season alignment.** Phasing assumes a 2026/27 PSL kick-off in late-July 2026; if the launch shifts the timing weights move with it.

## Validation post-approval
Once a tier is approved, the following must be re-validated before any spend commits:
1. Final rate-card pricing with each named supplier (DMS, Mediamark, OnsideZA, Polygon, SABC).
2. Vodacom and Toyota co-fund line items (treated as co-funded in the plan, not net new spend).
3. Trade footprint — actual list of priority Vodacom and independent dealer locations by province.
4. Stadium activation contracts with KC Stadium Ops for fan-zone and concourse rights.
5. Compliance / regulatory review of competition mechanics (port-in incentives, prize draws).
6. Measurement spine — confirmation that the MVNO core system can deliver weekly activation feeds.
